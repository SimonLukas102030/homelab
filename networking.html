Networking

Two-zone network: rack (bedroom) + living room, connected via uplink. WAP for mobile devices. Cisco SF300 moves to the barn for PoE cameras.

FLOOR PLANRoom Overview — Network & Audio

TOPOLOGYNetwork Topology

SWITCHESRack Switch + Living Room Switch

TP-Link TL-SG2210P ~75 €

8× Gbit PoE (powers WAP without a separate PSU), 2× SFP for future fibre option, VLAN/QoS managed. Sits on a 1U shelf in the rack.
Geizhals: TP-Link TL-SG2210P price comparison

TP-Link TL-SG108 ~15 €

8× Gbit, no PoE, no management — simple and cheap. More than enough for the living room (TV, Apple, music setup, spare). Connects to the rack switch via a standard Cat6 cable.
Amazon: amazon.de — TL-SG108
💡
SFP Ports: The 2× SFP on the rack switch can later be used for a fibre connection to the living room (LC-LC Duplex 10m ~12 €, SFP modules ~10 € each). FritzBox has no LAN SFP → router uplink stays standard RJ45.

WIFIAccess Point

TP-Link EAP670 ~80 €

WiFi 6 (AX3000), PoE-powered (no PSU), Omada Controller runs free on Proxmox as a Docker container. Ceiling or wall mount for best coverage.
Budget option: EAP615 (~50 €, WiFi 6 AX1800) if budget is tight
Omada Controller: Docker image on Proxmox — runs as LXC, no extra hardware needed
✓
PoE Trick: The WAP is powered directly from the rack switch — just one cable (data + power). No PSU, no extra power outlet needed.

CISCOSF300-48p → Barn

🏚
Cisco SF300-48p leaves the rack. 48 ports sound good, but only 4× Gigabit — the rest is 100 Mbit. For PoE cameras in the barn that's ideal: 100 Mbit is plenty for IP cameras and PoE is already there. In the homelab rack it would just waste space.
New role
Barn — PoE cameras (100 Mbit is sufficient)
Rack replacement
TL-SG2210P (8× Gbit PoE + 2× SFP)
Benefit
All rack devices on Gigabit, WAP via PoE, no oversized switch in the rack
