Where & how to buy

Online stores, used marketplaces, refurbished servers, search strategies. Which platform for which component — and what to watch out for when buying used.

01New — Online Stores

Geizhals.de Price Comparison

First stop. Not a shop itself, but a price comparison across all German retailers. Set up a price alert (Geizhals account → "Watch" list) — you get an email as soon as the price drops below your threshold. Free.

Tip: Filter "in stock only" + "Mindfactory / Alternate / Amazon" narrows it down to reputable retailers. Don't just click the first price — often retailer #2 for €5 more is significantly better on service.

Mindfactory.de Components

Best price-to-performance pick for CPU, motherboard, RAM, SSD, PSU. Fast shipping, Mindfactory MidnightShopping sales (seasonal, often mid-Q4) are aggressive. Watch out: occasional B-stock hidden in listings — look for "Mindstar" items (discounted, often outlet).

Alternate.de Premium Components

Slightly more expensive than Mindfactory, but better B2C experience: faster RMA process, "Outlet" section (tested returns with 1-year warranty, often 15–25% cheaper). Good for PSU, case, coolers — components where you want warranty coverage.

Amazon.de Fast & Flexible

Prime = next day. Prices often worse than Mindfactory, but returns are best-in-class. Good for things you're unsure about (will the cooler fit the case?). Warning: Marketplace sellers (not "Sold and shipped by Amazon") can ship fakes — especially NVMe drives.

NBB / notebooksbilliger.de Deals

Despite the name, they sell everything. Deals are often insane (Black Friday, April sales). Subscribe to the newsletter — spam is manageable.

📅
Seasonal lows: Black Friday / Cyber Monday (late November), Prime Day (July), Mindfactory MidnightShopping (sporadic, ~10 PM announced). Watch components on Geizhals 6–8 weeks beforehand — if the price is already dropping before BF, Black Friday won't be anything special.

02Used — Marketplaces

eBay-Kleinanzeigen / Kleinanzeigen.de Best Used Source

For components where price > warranty: CPU, RAM, case, HDD (carefully), coolers, fans. Not for PSU (a dying PSU can kill everything else) or NVMe (lifespan hard to verify).

Search strategy:

  • Precise search term: "Ryzen 7 5700X" without quotes, then filter Shipping available + Private sellers (commercial = 19% VAT baked into price).
  • Search radius: local first (50 km, self-pickup = €0 shipping), then expand if nothing nearby.
  • Save search (bell icon) → push notification on new listings. Listings < 30 min old = often a real bargain, > 7 days = price is clearly too high.
  • Sort: "Newest first" — old listings are often already gone or overpriced.

Negotiating: Standard is −10 to −15%. Write politely, give a concrete reason (shipping, self-pickup, quick payment). Lowball offers (−40%) get ignored.

Payment: PayPal "Goods & Services" (buyer protection, costs the seller ~3% — hence often "Friends & Family only" → that's a risk). For local pickup: cash or bank transfer after testing.

What to ask: Purchase date, original packaging & receipt (= remaining warranty!), reason for selling, for CPU: no bent pins (ask for photo), for RAM: model number (branded RAM ≠ no-name), for HDD: ask for SMART values.

eBay (the auction site) Auction + Buyer Protection

For rare items that don't show up on Kleinanzeigen, or for auctions (sniping just before end = often cheaper). eBay takes buyer protection seriously — if something arrives broken, you get your money back. But: factor in shipping costs (often €5–15), and seller prices are higher than on Kleinanzeigen.

Tip: "Buy It Now + Best Offer" listings — sellers often accept 70–80% of the listed price if the listing has been up for a week.

Hardwareluxx / ComputerBase Marketplace Enthusiasts

Forum marketplace with user reputation. Sellers are usually tech enthusiasts who know exactly what they have. Prices are fairly reasonable, almost no lemons. Registration required. Good for: high-end components, Gigabyte AORUS motherboards, custom water-cooling parts.

⚠
Used-Buying NEVER: Power supplies (PSU) — an aging PSU can take the whole system with it when it dies. NVMe SSDs without SMART values (check the lifetime counter). Fans > 3 years old (worn bearings, will hum). HDDs without SMART output. Motherboards without original accessories (often bent pins in the socket).

03Refurbished Server Vendors

ServerShop24.de DE / Tower

German company, refurbished Dell PowerEdge / HP ProLiant tower servers with warranty. T340 with Xeon E-2236 + 32 GB ECC + 2× 1 TB can be found around €600–750. Fast shipping, straightforward RMA. But: for bedroom use you'll need a fan mod — the original tower fans are loud even in T-series models.

Bargain Hardware UK / Wholesale

UK-based, good for 1U/2U rack servers (HP DL360 / Dell R630). Focuses on bulk/B2B but individual sales are possible. Shipping to DE works, but factor in customs & Brexit fees. Warning: rack servers in a bedroom are a noise disaster.

NewServerLife / ServerMonkey (US) Global Market

Larger selection, often cheaper per component, but: expensive shipping, voltage converters for US PSUs, customs fees. For 99% of cases the DE market has the same stuff — stick with local.

3.5Dell PowerEdge — Used Purchase Specifics

Your main purchase — the server itself. Traded on eBay and Kleinanzeigen, often from companies refreshing their server fleet. What to check:

R720 / R730 / R740 — what to verify before buying

  • Service Tag — enter it at dell.com/support → full original configuration visible (which disks, which RAID controller, purchase date, is warranty still active?)
  • iDRAC license: Express (free, no KVM-over-IP) or Enterprise (remote KVM, ~€100 if purchased separately). Ask the seller explicitly which one is active.
  • RAID controller: PERC H310 / H710 / H730. For Proxmox ZFS you want HBA mode (IT mode). The H710 can be flashed — often already done at time of sale, but ask explicitly.
  • RAM slot population: ask for a photo of the interior. Some sellers only populate 4 of 24 slots — RAM expansion is more expensive later if the kit slots are inconsistent.
  • Caddies included?: Drive caddies (HDD tray rails) are expensive individually (~€15 each). Seller should include all 8.
  • iDRAC reset: seller should reset iDRAC to factory defaults before shipping — otherwise you have their account in there.
  • PSU count: 1× or 2× redundant? For a GPU build you want 2× 1100 W.
  • GPU riser bracket (for Medium / Pro): ask for a photo of the GPU slot area. Standard R730 has no GPU riser — it's an optional add-on.

GPU Cards — Tesla P40 / RTX 3090 Used Sources

  • Tesla P40 24 GB: eBay (DE/EU) ~€200–280. Search term: "Tesla P40". Very popular among LLM hobbyists, price fluctuates. Alibaba / Chinese resellers often cheaper (~€170) — but 4 weeks shipping, customs, no buyer protection.
  • RTX 3090 24 GB: Kleinanzeigen / eBay ~€600–750. Search term: "RTX 3090". 24 GB variant, NOT 3090 Ti (regular 3090 is better due to lower TDP). For used: mining history is problematic — ask about fan condition, hot-spot values (Furmark test).
  • RTX 3060 12 GB: Kleinanzeigen ~€200–260. Search term: "RTX 3060 12GB" (NOT the 8 GB variant!).

Rack Frames — Used Sources

  • Kleinanzeigen: Search "19 Zoll Rack", "Server Rack 12U". Often self-pickup only because heavy (15U closed: ~30 kg). Locally you can find up to 70% discount vs. new.
  • eBay: Triton, StarTech, APC, IBM. APC NetShelter models often have built-in PDUs (bonus).
  • New: Reichelt, NWMall, Geizhals → 9U/12U open frame ~€140–250 new.

PDU Used Market

  • APC AP7900B (Switched, 8 outlets): eBay €80–150. Check firmware version (AOS >= 6.4 for modern web UI), battery age (internal RAM backup battery ~5 years).
  • APC AP8941 (Switched + Metered): eBay €180–250. Better web UI, per-outlet power logging.
  • Brennenstuhl Premium-Line (manually switchable, 8-way with individual switches): new ~€40 — cheapest option, but no remote control.

04Checklists

Before paying for used hardware

  • Model number + photo of original box
  • Receipt & purchase date (remaining warranty!)
  • SMART values (HDDs, NVMe — smartctl -a)
  • For CPU: photo of socket pins (bent pins?)
  • For RAM: exact model number (no no-name)
  • For mobo: POST test photo, BIOS version
  • For PSU: Skip — never buy used PSU
  • For fans: age, bearing noise (test video)
  • Shipping method: DHL insured, not Hermes parcel

Negotiation levers

  • Offer self-pickup (seller saves on packaging)
  • Instant payment via PayPal F&F (your risk, but strongest lever)
  • Multiple components from the same seller = bundle discount
  • Polite, brief, with a concrete number: "Would you accept €X?"
  • Geizhals screenshot of lowest new price → "70% of new for used would be fair"
  • Time is on your side — if a listing has been up 7 days, the seller will come down
